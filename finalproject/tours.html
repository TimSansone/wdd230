Tours Offered

Punta Sur Beach Eco Beach Park

The Punta Sur Eco Beach Park is a popular eco-tourism located on the southern tip of Cozumel, an island off the eastern of Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula. The park features stunning natural scenery, including pristine beaches, coastal lagoons, and diverse wildlife. Visitors can explore the park's ecological attractions, such as the Celarain Lighthouse, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding. The park also includes a crocodile observation area, a maritime museum, and opportunities for snorkeling and diving in the vibrant coral reefs. Additionally, Punta Sur Eco Beach is known for its commitment to environmental conservation and sustainable tourism practices.

Mr. Sanchos Beach Club

Mr. Sanchos Beach Club is a popular beach destination located in Cozumel, Mexico. It is known for its vibrant and lively atmosphere, offering a range of amenities and activities for visitors. The beach club features a beautiful stretch of coastline with white sand and clear waters, providing the perfect setting for relaxation and water-based activities. Visitors to Mr. Sanchos can enjoy a variety of services and facilities, including beachfront bars and restaurants serving delicious Mexican cuisine and refreshing drinks. The beach club also offers water sports activities such as snorkeling, kayaking, and parasailing, providing opportunities for adventure and excitement.

Passion Island

Isla Pasion is a secluded island paradise in the Caribbean. Located just ten minutes from Cozumel by boat, Isla Pasion is the ultimate beach vacation spot. Take a dip in the crystal-clear waters, lounge in a hammock, go out to sea in a kayak, and spot some stingrays. The best time to visit the island is between November and March, when the weather is more pleasant. You have to buy an all-inclusive day pass or package tour to visit the island. It’s a popular destination for weddings.

Cozumel Pearl Farm

The Cozumel Pearl Farm, also known as the "Pearl Farm Cozumel," is a unique attraction in Cozumel, Mexico. It offers visitors an opportunity to learn about the process of pearl cultivation and experience the beauty of cultured pearls. At the Cozumel Pearl Farm, guests can embark on guided tours that provide insight into the cultivation of pearls, from the initial seeding of oysters to the harvest of the pearls themselves. The tour typically includes demonstrations of pearl extraction techniques and explanations of the intricate process involved in pearl farming. Additionally, visitors have the chance to admire the stunning variety of pearls produced at the farm and learn about their cultural and commercial significance.

Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza is a world-renowned archaeological site located on the Yucatán Peninsula in Mexico. This UNESCO World Heritage site is a significant remnant of the ancient Mayan civilization and is celebrated for its architectural and historical importance. The site features a collection of well-preserved structures, including the iconic El Castillo, also known as the Temple of Kukulcan, which is a magnificent step-pyramid that served as a temple to the Mayan deity Kukulcan. Additionally, Chichen Itza encompasses the Great Ball Court, the Temple of the Warriors, the Observatory (El Caracol), and the Sacred Cenote (a natural sinkhole), among other remarkable buildings and landmarks.

Tulum Mayan Ruins

The Tulum Ruins are an ancient archaeological site located on the eastern coast of the Yucatán Peninsula in Mexico. This UNESCO World Heritage site is renowned for its well-preserved Mayan ruins and stunning coastal location overlooking the Caribbean Sea. The Tulum Ruins are famous for their iconic 13th-century stone walls and structures, including the Castle (El Castillo), the Temple of the Frescoes, and the Temple of the Descending God. The site also features a network of roads and buildings that offer insight into the architectural and cultural achievements of the ancient Mayan civilization.
